Common Questions

Sealcoating questions, answered

Everything Boise homeowners and property managers ask before booking a sealcoat.

For most Boise driveways and lots, every 2-3 years keeps your asphalt protected and looking fresh. Our Idaho freeze-thaw winters are hard on pavement, so a regular sealcoat is the cheapest way to add years of life and avoid expensive repaving.

Sealcoat forms a protective barrier against UV oxidation, water penetration, oil and gas spills, and freeze-thaw cracking. It restores that deep black finish while sealing the surface so water can't get into cracks and destroy the base underneath.

Most driveways are ready for foot traffic in a few hours and vehicle traffic within 24-48 hours, depending on temperature and humidity. We'll give you exact timing for your job so the fresh sealcoat cures perfectly before you pull back in.

Always. We clean and hot-fill all cracks with flexible rubberized sealant first, then apply the sealcoat. Skipping crack repair lets water keep working under the surface - so proper prep is the difference between a job that lasts and one that doesn't.
